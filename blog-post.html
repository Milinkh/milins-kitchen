Enhancing the Dining Experience: 3 Simple Changes

Published on August 11, 2024 by Milin Khunkhun

Dining out is one of my favorite experiences. It's not just about the food but the overall atmosphere, the service, and the little details that make the outing special. Over time, I've noticed a few aspects that could be refined to further enhance the dining experience. Here are my top three suggestions for improving the experience when ordering at restaurants.

Provide Plates and Cutlery After Ordering

One thing I’ve observed is that menus are often placed directly on top of the plates and cutlery as soon as we sit down. While this might seem like a minor detail, it can feel less than ideal from a cleanliness perspective. Menus are handled by many people throughout the day, and placing them on the plates where food will soon be served might not be the best practice.

A small adjustment that could make a big difference would be to serve the plates and cutlery after the order has been taken. This way, the table remains uncluttered, and diners can enjoy their meal knowing that their plates have remained untouched and clean until it's time to eat.

Bring Back Physical Menus

During the COVID-19 pandemic, digital menus accessed via QR codes became a common practice. This was a necessary and understandable adaptation at the time, and for quick-service restaurants, it still makes a lot of sense due to its efficiency. However, when it comes to sit-down dining, there’s something special about holding a physical menu.

The tactile experience of flipping through a menu, feeling the pages, and taking in the design all contribute to the enjoyment of dining out. In restaurants with dim lighting, navigating a bright digital screen can detract from the ambiance, much like using a phone in a movie theater. A physical menu adds to the overall atmosphere, making the dining experience feel more authentic and enjoyable.

Maintain Pristine Menus

The condition of a menu can say a lot about a restaurant’s attention to detail and commitment to the dining experience. A menu that is clean, crisp, and easy to read sets a positive tone right from the start. On the other hand, menus that are sticky, stained, or have fading text can unintentionally send the message that details are being overlooked.

Taking care to keep menus in good condition—whether they are paper, laminated, or otherwise-reflects a restaurant's dedication to providing a high-quality experience for its guests. It’s a small touch, but it shows that the restaurant values its customers and wants to make every aspect of their visit enjoyable.
